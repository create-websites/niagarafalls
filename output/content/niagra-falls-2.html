Nature's masterpiece

The Three Majestic Falls

Location: Straddling the international border between Ontario, Canada, and New York, USA, Niagara Falls is situated on the Niagara River, which connects Lake Erie to Lake Ontario.

Three Waterfalls: It's not one, but three magnificent waterfalls. The mighty Horseshoe Falls (or Canadian Falls) is the largest, with the American Falls and the smaller Bridal Veil Falls located on the U.S. side. Goat Island majestically separates the Horseshoe Falls from the other two.

By the Numbers:

  • Horseshoe Falls: ~57 meters (~187 ft) high and ~790 meters wide.
  • American Falls: ~21 to 30 meters high (due to boulders at its base) and ~320 meters wide.

Top Attractions

Maid of the Mist

Get up close and personal with the falls on this world-famous boat tour. Feel the spray and hear the thunderous roar from the base of the Horseshoe Falls.

Cave of the Winds

Journey behind the Bridal Veil Falls. Clad in a souvenir poncho, stand on the "Hurricane Deck" just feet from the torrent of water.

Skylon Tower

Soar 775 feet above the falls for breathtaking 360-degree views of the entire Niagara region from the observation deck.

The Story of the Falls

Glacial Origins: The falls are a relatively young geological feature, formed around 12,000 years ago as the last ice age ended. Melting glaciers unleashed massive volumes of water that carved the path of the Niagara River.

Rock Layers & Erosion: The falls are constantly, slowly moving upstream. This is due to a hard, erosion-resistant cap rock of limestone sitting atop softer layers of shale and sandstone, which are worn away by the powerful water.

Fascinating Facts

  • Emerald Green Water: The striking color isn't from pollution! It comes from finely ground rock particles (rock flour) and dissolved minerals carried by the river.
  • Slowing Erosion: The natural erosion rate has been significantly reduced due to water diversion for hydroelectric power.
  • Daredevil History: In 1901, 63-year-old Annie Edson Taylor became the first person to survive going over the falls in a barrel.
  • Night Illumination: The falls are beautifully illuminated with colorful lights every night, creating a spectacular sight after dusk.
