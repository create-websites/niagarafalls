The Incredible History of Niagara Falls

From its violent birth in the Ice Age to its status as a global icon in the age of Instagram.

12,000 Years Ago: A Violent Birth

As the last Ice Age ended, massive glaciers retreated, carving out the Great Lakes. The meltwater formed the Niagara River, which plunged over the steep Niagara Escarpment, giving birth to the Falls.

Pre-1600s: Home of the "Thundering Waters"

For centuries, the Falls were home to Indigenous peoples, including the Iroquois. The name "Niagara" is believed to originate from the Iroquois word "Onguiaahra," meaning "The Strait" or "Thundering Waters."

1678: European Arrival

French priest Father Louis Hennepin was the first European to document the Falls, describing them in his book with awe and wonder. His writings introduced Niagara to the Western world, sparking immense curiosity.

Early 1800s: The Rise of Tourism

With the arrival of railways, Niagara became an accessible tourist destination. It quickly gained a reputation as the "Honeymoon Capital of the World," a tradition that continues to this day.

1901: The Daredevil Era

On her 63rd birthday, Annie Edson Taylor became the first person to survive a trip over the Falls in a barrel. Her stunt kicked off an era of daredevils attempting to conquer the mighty cataract.

Late 1800s: Harnessing the Power

Innovators like Nikola Tesla saw the potential of the Falls for hydroelectric power. The first large-scale power plants were built, transforming industry and illuminating cities hundreds of miles away.

1885: A New Era of Preservation

Worried about rampant commercialization, conservationists on both sides of the border established parks (Niagara Falls State Park in the U.S. and Queen Victoria Park in Canada) to protect the natural beauty for future generations.

Today: An Instagram Icon

Millions of visitors from around the globe now visit each year, capturing its beauty not just in their memories but on their social media feeds, ensuring its status as a timeless and iconic natural wonder.
