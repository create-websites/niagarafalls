The Best Viewpoints of Niagara Falls

An exploration of stunning vistas, including famous viewpoints, secret hideaways, and beloved local gems.

Views from the Canadian Side

Table Rock Welcome Centre

Experience the iconic power of standing mere feet away from the edge of the colossal Horseshoe Falls, feeling the deafening roar and refreshing mist enveloping you. It is the ultimate immersive view from ground level.

Queen Victoria Park

The iconic Niagara Parkway promenade provides a stunning panoramic view of both the American and Horseshoe Falls, making it ideal for a leisurely stroll any time of day or night.

The Niagara Parkway

Winston Churchill once dubbed this as 'the most beautiful Sunday drive in the entire world.' As you travel north of the falls, you will find plenty of pull-off spots that provide distinct and less crowded views.

Views from the U.S. Side

Prospect Point & Observation Tower

This area on the U.S. side provides the best view of the American Falls, with the Observation Tower extending over the gorge for a clear panorama.

Goat Island & Terrapin Point

Experience the power up close at Goat Island, where you can explore different trails and viewpoints. Terrapin Point offers a thrilling perspective right next to the edge of the Horseshoe Falls.

Three Sisters Islands (Local Favorite)

Discover this hidden treasure by strolling over a chain of quaint bridges to reach the islands in the upper rapids. While the falls remain out of sight, you'll experience the awe-inspiring might of the river just before it cascades down.

Photography Pro Tips

Golden Hour is Magic

Photographing during the golden hours, either right after sunrise or just before sunset, enhances the mist's glow and increases the likelihood of spotting rainbows.

Use a Slow Shutter Speed

For a silky, smooth water effect, utilize a tripod and adjust your camera to a slow shutter speed (such as 1/2 second or longer) with the assistance of a neutral density (ND) filter for bright lighting conditions.

Incorporate Leading Lines

Utilize railings, pathways, or the riverbank to guide the viewer's gaze towards the waterfall, creating depth and enhancing the visual appeal of your composition.

Protect Your Gear

The mist is pervasive! Remember to bring a lens cloth to keep your lens clear and think about using a rain sleeve for your camera if you're getting up close.
