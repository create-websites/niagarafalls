Daredevils of Niagara

The daring tales of individuals who dared to defy the Falls stories of daring feats, resilience, and heartbreak.

October 24, 1901

Annie Edson Taylor: The First to Survive

For her 63rd birthday, teacher Annie Edson Taylor attempted to gain fame and wealth by enclosing herself in a specially made oak and leather barrel and plunging over the Horseshoe Falls. After 17 minutes, she emerged, injured but alive, and famously cautioned against repeating the dangerous stunt.

July 25, 1911

Bobby Leach: The First Man

A decade after Taylor's feat, English stuntman Bobby Leach attempted the plunge in a custom steel barrel. He survived but endured a six-month hospital stay for two broken kneecaps and a fractured jaw. Tragically, 15 years later, he passed away from an infection after slipping on an orange peel in New Zealand.

July 9, 1960

Roger Woodward: The Accidental Survivor

Not everyone who went over the falls were daredevils. In a boating mishap, 7-year-old Roger Woodward went over the Horseshoe Falls with just a lifejacket. Miraculously, he was seen in the turbulent water and saved by the Maid of the Mist crew, making him the sole survivor of such a dangerous plunge.

A History of Tragedy

Although some have managed to survive, numerous others have lost their lives trying to go over the Falls. These risky feats are now prohibited, with severe penalties and the threat of incarceration. The Falls should be revered as a natural marvel, not as a dare to be taken on.
