Anatomy of a Natural Wonder

A Triptych of Power

Niagara Falls consists of three separate waterfalls, with the powerful Horseshoe Falls dominating the flow and overshadowing the smaller American falls.

Flow Distribution

The Horseshoe Falls handles around 90% of the Niagara River's flow, channeling vast erosive force along its curved edge.

By the Numbers: Height & Width

Though the American Falls has a steeper drop to its rocky base, the Horseshoe Falls stands taller, stretches wider, and flows more powerfully.

A 12,000-Year Retreat

Born of melting glaciers, the Falls have steadily eroded their path upstream over millennia, leaving behind the 11km (7-mile) Niagara Gorge.

12,000 Years Ago: The Birth

The Niagara Escarpment near today's Lewiston, NY, shaped the Falls as glacial meltwater started cascading over the edge.

Pre-1900s: Natural Erosion

The Falls erode quickly, retreating about 3 to 5 feet annually, endlessly altering the terrain.

20th Century: Human Intervention

Massive water diversion for hydroelectric energy significantly reduces erosion to nearly 1 foot annually.

The Future: 50,000 Years?

Left unchecked, geologists predict the Falls could erode to Lake Erie in 50,000 years, fully draining the lake.

Taming the Giant

The force that evokes awe also serves as a vast energy source. The 1950 Niagara River Water Diversion Treaty strikes a fine balance between natural beauty and electricity production.
