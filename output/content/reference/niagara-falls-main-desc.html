A Triptych of Power

Niagara Falls is not simply a single waterfall, but a stunning trio of waterfalls located at the southern tip of Niagara Gorge. Straddling the border between the United States and Canada, these waterfalls boast the highest collective flow rate of any waterfall in North America.

Horseshoe Falls (Canadian Side)

The biggest and strongest, accounting for approximately 90% of the river's flow.

American Falls

Famous for its traditional straight crest and the large stack of rocks (talus) at the bottom.

Bridal Veil Falls

The tiniest of the trio, situated apart from the American Falls by Luna Island.

Unforgettable Things To Do

Maid of the Mist & Hornblower

Experience the iconic Niagara adventure. Venture into the misty core of the Horseshoe Falls and be enveloped by its incredible strength. A must-see attraction on both sides of the border.

Cave of the Winds (USA)

Go down into the gorge and step onto the 'Hurricane Deck,' just a few feet away from the roaring Bridal Veil Falls, for an invigorating and soaking wet adventure.

Journey Behind the Falls (Canada)

Discover century-old tunnels that guide you to viewing platforms situated right behind the roaring cascade of the Horseshoe Falls, offering a one-of-a-kind vantage point.

Plan Your Perfect Trip

Best Time to Visit

While stunning year-round, each season offers a unique experience:

  • Summer:Warmest weather, all attractions open, but largest crowds.
  • Fall:Pleasant weather, fewer crowds, and beautiful autumn foliage.
  • Winter:A magical frozen wonderland with unique ice formations.
  • Spring:Powerful flow from melting snow as the region awakens.

Where to Stay & View

For the best experience, reserve a 'Fallsview' room on the Canadian side for direct, panoramic views of both waterfalls.

  • Best Ground View (CAN): Table Rock Centre is situated right on the edge of the Horseshoe Falls.
  • Best Ground View (USA): Prospect Point offers dramatic views alongside the American Falls.
  • Insider Tip: Remember to bring your passport! The scenery is unique and worth exploring on both sides of the border.

Legends & Daredevils

The Legend of the Maid of the Mist

Uncover the age-old Haudenosaunee tale of Lelawala, a youthful maiden who ventured into the swift currents and was ensnared by the thunder deity Hinum, residing within a cavern hidden behind the cascading waters. Her essence is believed to linger on, eternally remembered as the Maid of the Mist.

A History of Daredevils

The fame of The Falls has always drawn daring individuals. Annie Edson Taylor, aged 63 at the time, made history in 1901 as the first person to survive going over the falls in a barrel. Seeking fame and fortune, she cautioned against others attempting to replicate her risky stunt.
