A Triptych of Power

Niagara Falls is not simply a single waterfall, but rather a stunning trio of waterfalls located at the southern tip of Niagara Gorge. Straddling the United States and Canada border, they boast the highest collective flow rate of any waterfall in North America.

Horseshoe Falls (Canadian Side)

The biggest and strongest, holding approximately 90% of the river's flow.

American Falls

Famous for its traditional straight crest and the large pile of rocks (talus) at the bottom.

Bridal Veil Falls

The tiniest of the trio, positioned apart from the American Falls by Luna Island.

Unforgettable Things To Do

Maid of the Mist & Hornblower

Experience the iconic essence of Niagara at the Horseshoe Falls, where you can immerse yourself in the mist and feel the incredible force of nature. A must-see attraction on both sides of the border.

Cave of the Winds (USA)

Explore the gorge and step onto the 'Hurricane Deck' where you can feel the spray of Bridal Veil Falls just a few feet away, providing a thrilling and soaking experience.

Journey Behind the Falls (Canada)

Discover ancient tunnels that are over a century old, granting access to observation points located just behind the roaring power of the Horseshoe Falls for an unparalleled view.

Plan Your Perfect Trip

Best Time to Visit

While stunning year-round, each season offers a unique experience:

  • Summer:Warmest weather, all attractions open, but largest crowds.
  • Fall:Pleasant weather, fewer crowds, and beautiful autumn foliage.
  • Winter:A magical frozen wonderland with unique ice formations.
  • Spring:Powerful flow from melting snow as the region awakens.

Where to Stay & View

To truly enhance your stay, reserve a 'Fallsview' room for unparalleled views of both waterfalls from the Canadian side.

  • Best Ground View (CAN): Table Rock Centre places you directly beside the edge of the Horseshoe Falls.
  • Best Ground View (USA): Prospect Point offers dramatic views alongside the American Falls.
  • Insider Tip: Remember to bring your passport! The perspectives are unique and worth experiencing from each side of the border.

Legends & Daredevils

The Legend of the Maid of the Mist

Explore the age-old Haudenosaunee tale of Lelawala, a brave young woman who ventured into the rapids and was ensnared by the thunder spirit Hinum, dwelling in a cavern hidden behind the cascades. Her essence is believed to linger on, forever remembered as the Maid of the Mist.

A History of Daredevils

The fame of The Falls has always drawn daring individuals, like 63-year-old Annie Edson Taylor who, in 1901, became the first person to survive going over the falls in a barrel. Seeking fame and fortune, she cautioned others against attempting her dangerous stunt.
