A Triptych of Water

Not one waterfall, but three

Niagara is not just one waterfall - it consists of three separate cascades situated at the top of the Niagara Gorge, divided by Goat Island and the small Luna Island. The majority of the water flow is carried by the Canadian Horseshoe Falls, while the American and Bridal Veil falls run along the New York shoreline in a more direct, rocky formation.

Horseshoe Falls ≈ 90% of the flow

The massive colossus looms with a curved crest on the Canadian side, offering the best view from Table Rock where you can feel the thunderous roar in your chest.

American Falls US side

A traditional straight crest with a large pile of fallen rocks at its foot, seen from Prospect Point in Niagara Falls State Park.

Bridal Veil Falls smallest

The fragile one, isolated by Luna Island from the American Falls, encountered up close on the decks of the Cave of the Winds.

Ways to meet the water

As you approach the falls, you will be rewarded. The adult rates for 2026 listed below are subject to change, so be sure to verify current times and prices before your visit, as schedules may vary due to weather and season.

Boat Tour · US Side

Maid of the Mist

The classic ride has been revamped with all-electric boats departing from Niagara Falls State Park. Enjoy a 20-minute journey alongside the American Falls, ending in the misty spray at the base of Horseshoe. Complimentary blue ponchos provided.

≈ US$30.25 adult · season May–Oct Boat Tour · Canada Side

Niagara City Cruises

Niagara Parks' official operator (previously known as Hornblower) embarks near Table Rock, offering extended catamaran tours in iconic red ponchos. Experience evening Illumination and Fireworks cruises as well.

≈ C$47.95 adult +tax · season May–Dec Tunnels · Canada Side

Journey Behind the Falls

Explore tunnels that are over a hundred years old, leading to portals and a lower deck positioned right behind the roaring Horseshoe Falls.

Table Rock Centre · year-round Decks · US Side

Cave of the Winds

Every year, newly constructed wooden pathways guide you to the Hurricane Deck, just steps away from Bridal Veil Falls, where the mist strikes like a fierce tropical tempest.

Goat Island · seasonal Overlook

Skylon Tower & Rapids

Experience a 360° panoramic view from the glass elevator, or explore the wilder stretches downstream by walking the White Water Walk and riding the Whirlpool Aero Car.

After Dark

Colour, light, and fireworks

The falls are illuminated in LED colors every night of the year after sunset, with the sky joining in during the warm season with nightly five-minute fireworks displays. 2026 marks a special year as the Illumination Board celebrates its centennial, with World Cup-themed colors expected throughout the summer.

When to Go

Picking your season

The falls flow year-round, with each month offering a unique experience. Find a detailed month-by-month breakdown in the seasons guide.

SummerJun–Aug
Peak season offers the best of everything: sunny days, all attractions open, fireworks every night. However, be prepared for large crowds and higher prices. Plan ahead and arrive early to make the most of your visit.
FallSep–Nov
The expert's choice. September offers the perfect balance of cool air, fewer crowds post-Labour Day, and a vibrant parkway filled with autumn hues, making it the ideal weather-to-crowd ratio month of the year.
WinterDec–Feb
The parkway is a serene winter wonderland with ice-coated railings and the dazzling Winter Festival of Lights. While some attractions may be closed, the festive glow continues to shine brightly.
SpringMar–May
The snowmelt fuels a rapid increase in flow, marking the start of the season and bringing back fireworks by mid-May, just in time for the summer rush.

Insider tips

01Go on a weekday. Weekdays from Monday to Thursday are consistently more peaceful compared to weekends, regardless of the season.
02Bundle your tickets. The Niagara Falls Wonder Pass in Canada or the Discovery/Attractions Pass in the US offers a combination of major attractions and cost savings.
03Bring a passport. Understanding different perspectives involves traversing the Rainbow Bridge, where each side offers a unique viewpoint.
04Shoot the golden hour. During the golden hour, mist sparkles and rainbows often appear. Don't forget a lens cloth - the spray spreads easily.
05Stay for the night show. A Fallsview room offers a stunning view of the illuminated fireworks right outside your window.
History & Legend

The stories the water carries

Centuries before tour boats arrived, the falls held sacred significance and eventually became a platform for daring stunts throughout history.

Indigenous Culture

Onguiaahra the Thundering Waters

The Haudenosaunee considered the falls a sacred place, where the story of Lelawala, the Maid of the Mist, and Hinum the thunder spirit has been handed down through the ages.
