Nature's masterpiece

The Three Majestic Falls

Location: Niagara Falls is located on the Niagara River, bridging the border between Ontario, Canada, and New York, USA, connecting Lake Erie and Lake Ontario.

Three Waterfalls: It's not one, but three magnificent waterfalls. The mighty Horseshoe Falls (or Canadian Falls) is the largest, with the American Falls and the smaller Bridal Veil Falls Goat Island, located on the American side, grandly divides the Horseshoe Falls from the remaining two falls.

By the Numbers:

  • Horseshoe Falls: ~57 meters (~187 ft) high and ~790 meters wide.
  • American Falls: Rising approximately 21 to 30 meters tall (thanks to large boulders at its foundation) and spanning roughly 320 meters in width.

Top Attractions

Maid of the Mist

Experience the renowned boat tour that takes you right next to the falls. Encounter the mist and listen to the powerful sound of the Horseshoe Falls up close.

Cave of the Winds

Explore the area behind Bridal Veil Falls while wearing a souvenir poncho, and experience the power of the rushing water from the 'Hurricane Deck' just a few steps away.

Skylon Tower

Experience breathtaking 360-degree views of the entire Niagara region from the observation deck, soaring 775 feet above the falls.

The Story of the Falls

Glacial Origins: The falls were created approximately 12,000 years ago at the conclusion of the ice age when melting glaciers released large amounts of water, shaping the Niagara River.

Rock Layers & Erosion: The falls steadily climb upstream as the erosion-resistant limestone cap rock sits above the softer shale and sandstone layers, gradually worn away by the forceful water.

Fascinating Facts

  • Emerald Green Water: The vibrant hue is not caused by pollution, but by rock flour and dissolved minerals transported by the river.
  • Slowing Erosion: Water diversion for hydroelectric power has greatly decreased the natural erosion rate.
  • Daredevil History: Annie Edson Taylor, aged 63, made history in 1901 as the first person to successfully navigate the falls in a barrel and survive.
  • Night Illumination: Every night, the falls are adorned with vibrant lights, transforming the landscape into a stunning spectacle once darkness falls.
