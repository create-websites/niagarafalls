Daredevils of Niagara

The daring tales of individuals who dared to challenge the Falls stories of risky feats, resilience, and heartbreak.

October 24, 1901

Annie Edson Taylor: The First to Survive

For her 63rd birthday, teacher Annie Edson Taylor attempted to gain fame and wealth by locking herself in a specially made barrel of oak and leather. She was then pulled into the river and dropped over the Horseshoe Falls. After 17 minutes, she emerged battered but breathing. Taylor's famous advice: 'Never try that again.'

July 25, 1911

Bobby Leach: The First Man

A decade following Taylor's feat, English daredevil Bobby Leach ventured into the depths in a custom steel barrel. While he successfully survived the plunge, he endured a lengthy hospital stay due to two broken kneecaps and a fractured jaw. Tragically, 15 years later, he succumbed to an infection after slipping on an orange peel in New Zealand.

July 9, 1960

Roger Woodward: The Accidental Survivor

Among the group, not everyone was a daredevil. Seven-year-old Roger Woodward found himself going over the Horseshoe Falls after a boating mishap, clad only in a lifejacket. Miraculously, he was sighted in the turbulent waters beneath and saved by the Maid of the Mist boat crew, making him the sole individual to live through such a perilous descent.

A History of Tragedy

Although some have survived, numerous individuals have lost their lives attempting to traverse the Falls. These risky stunts are now prohibited and can result in severe penalties and potential incarceration. The Falls should be admired for their natural beauty, not viewed as a daring feat to be accomplished.
